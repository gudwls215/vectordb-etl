Caregiver Certification Process

  1. What is a caregiver?

    • Caregiving refers to the specific act of helping someone other than me, and a caregiver refers to a person who cares for a patient by completing a curriculum at a specific educational institution (nursing academy, hospital-affiliated medical institution, general corporation, etc.). A person who has acquired a qualification recognized by the Korea Institute for Nursing Development (Certification and Evaluation (Korea Private Qualification Association), a nursing qualification examination management institution for those who are engaged in such nursing activities or who wish to engage in such activities, shall be a caregiver.

  2. Performance work

    • They take care of and help the activities of patients, the elderly and the disabled at hospitals, nursing homes, related institutions, and homes.

    • Provide services such as bathing and dressing patients, the elderly, and the disabled with mobility difficulties.

    • Aid the patient's exercise by taking care of meals, snacks and medications, assisting with excretion

Process for obtaining a certificate of nursing care worker

  1. What is a nursing care worker?

    • This refers to a person who has completed a prescribed curriculum at an educational institution that trains nursing care workers and has obtained a qualification as a nursing care worker granted by the state after passing the national examination. This refers to a person who supports physical activities and daily life to adults who are unable to perform their daily life alone due to old age or senile diseases, etc., mainly at home visiting through living welfare facilities or home care services.
      (Source: Korean Standard Occupational Classification by Statistics Korea)

  2. Performance work

    • Nursing care workers specialize in performing tasks such as physical activities of the elderly, etc. or supporting domestic activities.

    • It collects information about the subject from doctors, nurses, and families, establishes a care protection service plan, maintains cleanliness of the subject, meals and medication assistance, excretion, exercise, emotional support, environmental management, and daily life support.

    • In addition, they not only take care of the person who needs nursing care services, but also maintain a posture with the person at all times, and seek specific advice on life support such as cleaning, washing, and cooking, physical assistance such as excretion, bathing, and eating, or difficulties in daily life.
      (Source: Article 39-2 of the Welfare of Older Persons Act, etc.)
