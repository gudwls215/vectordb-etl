CurriculumIntroducing EPS TOPIK

What is EPS TOPIK?

EPS TOPIK, an abbreviation for the 'Employment Permit System Test of Proficiency in Korea,' is a test that evaluates Korean proficiency. The test is for those who wish to work as foreign workers in Korea, and evaluates Korean communication skills and basic understanding needed for professional life in Korea.

Why do we need EPS TOPIK?

One of the requirements to obtain work permits in Korea is to prove one's Korean proficiency. EPS TOPIK is used as an important measure to check whether foreign residents are able to communicate with Korean society at work. In particular, it is an essential qualification for foreign workers who want to work in Korea.

How much should I acquire?

Although the EPS TOPIK test consists of various levels, it must pass at least a certain level in order to obtain a work permit. The passing standards vary by industry, and the level of Korean proficiency required mainly by manufacturing and construction industries is high. The test consists of reading and listening, and certain scores must be earned in each section.

How should I study?

  1. Learning Basic Korean: Start by learning everyday conversations and basic grammar.

  2. Occupational Korean Learning: Focus on learning vocabulary and expressions related to Korean workplace culture.

  3. Safety and Hygiene: Prevent safety accidents and learn about expressing emergency responses in case of accidents and hygiene for a healthy life.

  4. Test Practice: Learn a practical sense through past test papers or mock tests.

  5. Utilize Multimedia Materials: Use Korean dramas, news, and radio broadcasts to improve listening and familiarize yourself with the language that is actually used.

  6. Language exchange partner: Strengthen real-life Korean language skills through language exchange with Koreans. (KPAL provides a P2P advanced Korean self-learning platform.)

Evaluation method and criteria to determine a successful candidate

  • Evaluation method : relative evaluation

  • Criteria to determine a successful candidate : Scores above the lowest score per industry, Successful candidate determined in the order of merit within selecting (anticipating) number of people.

    1. Manufacturing Industry: 110 points or more out of 200 points

    2. Minor industries (construction, agricultural and livestock industries, fishing): 80 points or more out of 200

    3. Fishery special exception: 60 points or more out of 200 points
